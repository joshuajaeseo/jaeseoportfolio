DSGN 308

Make grocery bags stronger and sustainable.


From personal experience our team had identified weak paper grocery bags as a problem space, but the insights from grocers using the bags on a daily basis piqued our interest.

The product requirements of durability, sustainability, comfort, and economics were drawn from our surveys, user interviews, and patent research.

Through ideation our team created four prototypes.

The prototypes were tested against single bags and double bags: common use cases in the real world.

After finding a grocery load that was on the breaking point of a single bag, we subjected each type of bag to this circuit of activities.

The objective of the tests were to find the point of structural failure for each bag. The breaking points were then plotted.

From these tests two frontrunners emerged, but although the double-handle bags performed the best, the increased complexity in manufacturing led to sticker-handle bags as the final product.

If our team decides to pursue this project further, we have outlined the steps needed to be taken to take our product to market.
